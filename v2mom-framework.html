V2MOM — Salesforce's five-line alignment cascade

Marc Benioff drafted V2MOM on the back of an envelope at Salesforce's founding in 1999. Five questions every leader, team, and individual answers in one page. It is an alignment mechanism, not a feature-impact estimator — and being honest about that is the whole point.

Goal
Decide features by data-backed expected impact — choose by outcome, not by to-do list or opinion.

The honest headline: it's a one-page cascade, not a scoring formula

V2MOM doesn't tell you what to build. It tells you what you're trying to do, what you'll refuse to compromise on, how you'll do it, what's in the way, and how you'll know it worked — written by every leader, cascaded to every team. The discipline is that everyone in the company can read the CEO's V2MOM and write a coherent one of their own that lines up with it.

Benioff's claim, repeated for 25 years: V2MOM is "the secret to Salesforce's success." Useful precisely because it doesn't pretend to estimate per-feature impact — it forces clarity at the level above.

The five letters

Every V2MOM answers the same five questions in the same order. The ordering is load-bearing: you can't write a sensible "Method" until you've written the "Values" that constrain it.

V
Vision
What do we want to achieve? One sentence, ambitious, unambiguous.
V
Values
What principles guide the work? What we will not compromise on.
M
Methods
How will we get there? The 3–5 strategic actions.
O
Obstacles
What stands in the way? Honest naming of risks & gaps.
M
Measures
How will we know we succeeded? Numeric outcomes.
Source: Salesforce — Create alignment with a V2MOM · Benioff's Behind the Cloud (2009) tells the origin story (envelope, American Airlines flight, March 1999).

How V2MOM actually picks the work

It picks strategic direction, not features. The mechanism is the cascade: CEO writes one, every leader writes one aligned to it, every team writes one aligned to their leader's, every individual writes one aligned to their team's. Misalignment is visible in writing.

1

CEO writes the company V2MOM first

Single page. Vision is one sentence. Methods are the 3–5 strategies — these are what the rest of the company must align to.

2

Each org/team writes their own V2MOM that ladders up

The team's Vision is a slice of the CEO's; the team's Methods support one of the CEO's Methods. If a team's V2MOM doesn't connect, that's a misalignment to debate.

3

Individuals write personal V2MOMs aligned to their team

Everyone, every year. Salesforce makes this part of performance review. The cumulative effect: each person can point at how their work ladders to the CEO's Vision.

4

Measures get tracked. Methods get revised.

Periodically — quarterly at most companies that use V2MOM — you check Measures and re-write the V2MOM. It is not static; it adapts as reality moves.

Worked example — Benioff's actual 1999 V2MOM

Reconstructed from Behind the Cloud and the Salesforce blog. The point isn't this exact wording — it's that the whole strategy of a company that became a $200B business fits on one page.

Salesforce, 1999 — the founding V2MOM (paraphrased from Benioff)

Vision
Rapidly create a world-class internet company / web site for sales force automation.
Values
World-class organization · Time to market · Functionality · Usability · Value to customer.
Methods
Hire the team · Finalize product specifications & technology · Build prototype · Run beta tests · Launch site.
Obstacles
Hiring engineering talent · Funding · Building rapidly with quality.
Measures
A working web site · Customers using it · Press coverage at launch.

Note the Measures: they are outcomes (working site, customers using it), not output counts (features shipped). And there is no per-feature impact estimate anywhere — that's the point.

What V2MOM does not do

It is not a prioritisation framework. A V2MOM tells your team which direction they're pushing in. It does not tell them whether to ship Feature A or Feature B next quarter. Inside a single "Method," you still need a scoring framework (RICE), an input model (North Star), or experimentation (ExP) to pick the bets that move the Measures.

It is not OKRs. OKRs are an objective + measurable key results, per cycle. V2MOM is a full strategic statement (vision, values, obstacles too) — many companies use V2MOM at leadership level and OKRs at team level. (Asana's Pyramid of Clarity takes the OKR side of the same shape.)
Why V2MOM is on the list anyway. Every other framework on this site assumes you've already agreed what you're trying to do. V2MOM is the artifact that produces that agreement and forces it into writing. Without it, RICE scores a wishlist; with it, RICE scores candidates that all support the same Method.
