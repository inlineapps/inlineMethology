How to read this — and the one thing it makes obvious

The methods sort into five categories: Scoring Input modeling Experimentation Goal-laddering / Define-first Written discipline. None of them is sufficient alone. Scoring without a goal is theatre; experimentation without a metric is noise; goal-laddering without scoring is alignment but no priority.

The point of the table is to make the gaps obvious — which jobs does each method actually do, and which does it leave to a partner method? Real teams stack 2–3 methods that cover all the jobs. The "three coherent stacks" section below shows three viable combinations to compare and choose between.

The five categories — what each does, what it leaves out

Re-organising the table by category, so you can see which jobs each kind of method actually does. A complete stack covers all five (or deliberately skips one and admits it).

Scoring — turn many candidates into a coarse ranking

Methods: RICE (Intercom), Weighted Scoring (Atlassian). What they do: let you compare 50 ideas on the same scale and surface the top 5–10. The Confidence multiplier in RICE is the discipline — un-evidenced ideas are visibly weaker.

What they don't do: tell you what goal to score against, or estimate lift. Pair with goal-laddering (V2MOM / Pyramid) for goals, and with experimentation for lift.

Input modeling — model the metric, focus on the lever

Method: North Star Framework (Amplitude / Slack). What it does: writes the causal model NSM = f(inputs), picks the focus input, narrows the roadmap to bets on that input.

What it doesn't do: rank the bets that move the input. That falls to scoring (RICE) or experimentation. North Star is the filter, not the scoreboard.

Experimentation — measure causal impact rather than estimate it

Methods: Microsoft ExP, LinkedIn T-REX, Netflix, Booking, Airbnb ERF, Uber XP, DoorDash switchback, Lyft, Pinterest. What they do: A/B and quasi-experimental designs that produce a causal estimate of lift on a pre-declared OEC. The only category that produces ground-truth numbers.

What they don't do: work without traffic, replace metric design, or pick the bet. Pair with goal-laddering for what to test, scoring for which to test first.

Define-first / Goal-laddering — set the destination, force traceability

Methods: Amazon PR-FAQ, Google HEART/GSM, Spotify DIBB, Salesforce V2MOM, Asana Pyramid. What they do: establish what success looks like, and require every piece of work to trace upward to it. The discipline that stops "feature-itis."

What they don't do: rank features against each other, or measure outcomes. They're upstream of scoring and experimentation, not substitutes.

Written discipline — rigour by prose, not by formula

Method: Stripe product shaping. What it does: forces every bet into a deep-dive doc filtered by a multi-decade direction; quality of writing is the funding gate.

What it doesn't do: produce a number, or operate without an existing writing culture. The honest "no scoring formula" exemplar — included to make explicit that successful companies sometimes skip scoring deliberately.

Three coherent stacks you could actually adopt

Nineteen methods is overwhelming; you only need 3–4 that cover the bases. Here are three "complete" stacks — each picks one method per job, sourced from companies that actually use them together.

Stack A — Scored & cascaded

  1. Direction: V2MOM (Salesforce) — one-page cascade.
  2. Metric: HEART/GSM (Google) — pick the success metrics.
  3. Rank: RICE (Intercom) — coarse ranking of candidates.
  4. Audit: log estimate-vs-actual after each release.

Fits Route A. Cheap, fast, biased toward up-front estimation. Risk: confident-looking scores that turn out wrong.

Stack B — Measured & focused

  1. Direction: North Star (Amplitude/Slack) — pick the focus input.
  2. Bets: DIBB (Spotify) — every bet needs a data chain.
  3. Validate: Microsoft ExP-lite — ramp behind a flag with a pre-declared OEC.
  4. Audit: Uber-style holdout quarterly.

Fits Route B. Needs traffic/infra; produces ground-truth numbers. Risk: slow on contractual / no-traffic items.

Stack C — Hybrid (the synthesis)

  1. Direction: North Star + V2MOM-lite (combine model + cascade).
  2. Bet artifact: PR-FAQ for big bets; RICE for the long tail.
  3. Validate: ExP-lite ramp for discovery items; ship as scored for contracts.
  4. Audit: estimate-vs-actual + a small holdout cohort.

Fits the synthesis. More to learn; covers contract bets and discovery bets honestly.
