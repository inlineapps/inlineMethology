Amazon Working Backwards / PR-FAQ — write the launch before the code

A mock press release plus an FAQ — both written before a line of code is shipped. The discipline of the most influential pre-build estimation method in modern tech: if you can't write a compelling PR for the customer, you don't yet have a feature worth building.

Goal
Decide features by data-backed expected impact — choose by outcome, not by to-do list or opinion.

The honest headline: the strongest pre-build forcing function on the list

Most "estimation" methods score features after the team has decided what to build. PR-FAQ inverts the order: you write the customer-facing launch first. If the press release reads as boring, irrelevant, or implausible, you re-shape the problem — long before you've sunk engineering effort. Ex-Amazon execs Bryar & Carr (Working Backwards, 2021) call this the single most distinctive part of Amazon's culture.

It's the only method on this site that produces a readable artifact a non-PM can react to. That's why it travels well — but also why it's hard: writing one good PR-FAQ takes days, not minutes.

The artifact — what a PR-FAQ actually contains

Two parts, both short. The strict format is the discipline: limited length forces clarity.

PR — Press release (≤1 page)

Reads exactly like a real announcement

  • Headline — the product, in the language the customer would use.
  • Subheading — who it's for and the key benefit.
  • Summary — one paragraph the customer would understand.
  • Problem — the specific pain being solved (in customer's words).
  • Solution — how the product solves it.
  • Quote from a leader — captures the strategic intent.
  • How to get started — what the customer does next.
  • Customer quote — a hypothetical real reaction.
FAQ — Internal & external (≤5 pages)

The honest part — where edge cases get surfaced

  • External FAQ — questions a customer or journalist would ask: pricing, availability, comparison, limitations.
  • Internal FAQ — what the team needs to believe: market size, success metrics, dependencies, risks, who builds it.
  • Success metrics live here — explicit numeric outcomes ("we expect X% adoption in 6 months because…"). This is the line that makes PR-FAQ a pre-build impact estimator.
Source: About Amazon — culture & processes · Working Backwards (Bryar & Carr) — PR-FAQ process.

How a PR-FAQ actually picks the work

The mechanism is iterative and brutal: most first drafts get killed. Bezos's well-known rule: the PR-FAQ must be revised until the room can't poke a useful hole in it.

1

Pick the customer and the bad-day moment

Start from a real customer in a specific situation. The PR will be written for them, not for the org chart.

2

Draft the PR

Write the launch as if it ships tomorrow. If the headline is weak, the product is weak. The team often discovers in this step that the feature they wanted to build isn't compelling to the customer.

3

Write the FAQ — both halves

External FAQ surfaces customer objections; Internal FAQ surfaces team disagreements and forces numeric success metrics. This is where "expected impact" becomes a written number.

4

Review in a silent-read meeting

Amazon's distinctive ritual: the room reads the doc for 20 minutes in silence before discussion. No slides, no selling — the doc has to stand on its own. Most bets die here.

5

Iterate or kill

If the doc survives critique, work begins from the PR backwards — what would have to be built to make the PR true? Many candidates iterate the PR for weeks before any code is written.

Worked example — the original Kindle PR-FAQ (the famous story)

1-page PR, written before any Kindle hardware existed

Headline: "Introducing Kindle — every book ever printed, in any language, all available in under 60 seconds."

Subheading: Read books anywhere, instantly, without losing your library.

Key Internal-FAQ pressure tests it had to answer: Why would a reader pay for a device? How does it work without Wi-Fi (→ became free 3G)? What if publishers won't sign? What does success look like in year 1?

The Kindle PR-FAQ went through many iterations before the device was built. The team didn't just design the Kindle from the PR — they kept revising the PR until the customer story was undeniable.

Source for the Kindle PR-FAQ narrative: Bryar & Carr, Working Backwards, Chapter 5. The exact wording above is reconstructed from public retellings, not Amazon-published text — the structure and discipline are the verifiable parts.

Where PR-FAQ breaks

It's expensive. Days to weeks per artifact. Reserve PR-FAQs for big bets, not every small change. For routine work use RICE-style ranking.

It can ossify the product. If the PR was wrong, the team is anchored to it. Mitigation: explicit "kill criteria" in the Internal FAQ — what would convince us to stop?

It is not a substitute for experimentation. The PR-FAQ estimates expected impact; controlled experiments measure actual. Amazon does both — PR-FAQ at the bet level, A/B at the change level.
Why PR-FAQ is on the list. It's the only Consumer-side framework that produces an up-front written estimate of customer value rather than relying on post-launch measurement. The closest cousin in the SaaS set is Stripe's deep-dive memo, but PR-FAQ adds the strict format that makes it teachable.
